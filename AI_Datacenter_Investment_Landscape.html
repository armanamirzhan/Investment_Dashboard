Sector Overview

⚡ Electricity Supply

Status: Critical bottleneck. US grid queue at 2,600 GW backlog with 5-year median wait. Power transformer lead times at 128+ weeks. Half of planned US DC builds delayed or canceled due to power infrastructure shortages.

Key players: GE Vernova (100 GW turbine backlog), Constellation Energy (55 GW nuclear fleet), Vistra, NextEra, Eaton, Schneider Electric.

Investment themes: Behind-the-meter gas power, nuclear restarts and PPAs, SiC power electronics, transformer manufacturers, battery storage.

🔧 Hardware & Supply Chain

Status: Supply-constrained across the stack. Nvidia Blackwell sold out through mid-2026. HBM memory allocation fully booked. TSMC CoWoS packaging oversubscribed. All hyperscalers developing custom silicon to diversify.

Key players: Nvidia (GPUs), TSMC (fabrication), SK Hynix (HBM), Broadcom (custom ASICs/networking), Arista (switches), Vertiv (cooling), Corning (fiber).

Investment themes: Custom silicon race, HBM memory supercycle, liquid cooling shift, optical transceiver ramp (800G→1.6T), geopolitical supply chain diversification.

Grid Interconnection Queue

Severity: Critical. US queue at 2,600 GW. Median wait: ~5 years. Primary driver of behind-the-meter solutions.

Power Transformer Shortage

Severity: Critical. 30% supply deficit. Lead times: 128 weeks (power), 144 weeks (GSU). Half of planned US DC builds delayed.

Gas Turbine Backlog

Severity: High. GE Vernova sold out through 2030. Siemens backlog 5+ years. Order lead times pushing projects to 2028+.

HALEU Fuel Scarcity

Severity: Medium. Required for most SMRs. Centrus (LEU) only US commercial source. Could delay SMR deployments.

Regulatory & Permitting

Severity: Medium. Ireland, Netherlands blocking DC connections. Parts of Virginia restricting growth. NRC reviews 18+ months.

Demand Pullback Risk

Severity: Medium. Microsoft froze 1.5 GW self-build. Only ~5 GW of 16 GW promised for 2026 actually being built.

Power Delivery Infrastructure

Transformer Bottleneck

Critical constraint: More than half of planned US DC builds delayed/canceled. Lead times expanded from 24-30 months to 3-5 years. China imports surged from 1,500 to 8,000+ units. Investment by hyperscalers ($650B+ in 2026) constrained by transformer availability.

48V → 800V DC Architecture

OCP standardized on 48V busbar (1400A/segment) but this is reaching limits at 100kW+ racks. Vertiv 800V DC ecosystem coming H2 2026 (98% efficiency, up to 1.1 MW/rack). Mixed voltage environments expected for years during transition.

Busbar Adoption

~70% of new DC projects using busbars instead of cables. Supports loads up to 4,000 amps. Modular, reconfigurable — critical for evolving AI deployments. Higher initial capex but superior operational flexibility.

Optical Transceiver Speed Migration

Backend Network: InfiniBand vs Ethernet (New Deployments)

Co-Packaged Optics (CPO)

Nvidia Spectrum-X Photonics: Integrates silicon photonics directly onto ASIC. 5x power efficiency, 10x resiliency. Up to 409.6 Tb/s. Available H2 2026 from system vendors. Full-scale CPO deployment expected ~2028.

Ultra Ethernet Consortium (UEC)

UEC 1.0 spec released June 2025. 100+ member companies. First interop demo at OFC 2026 (Keysight + Broadcom at 800GE). ~70% of new AI deployments now choosing Ethernet over InfiniBand. RoCEv2 increasingly preferred.

China's Self-Sufficiency Push
