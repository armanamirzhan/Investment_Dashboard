# Electricity Infrastructure Companies for AI Datacenters - 2026 Deep Research

## Executive Summary

**Market Context:** U.S. datacenter electricity demand is expected to climb to 35 GW by 2030 (from 19 GW in 2023), with potential reaching 176 GW by 2035. This represents an unprecedented infrastructure buildout challenge across the entire electricity supply chain.

**Companies Identified:** 85+ significant companies mapped across 13 major categories

**Key Findings:**
- Nuclear utilities (Constellation, Vistra, NextEra) now central to AI infrastructure strategy
- Small modular reactors (NuScale, Oklo, Kairos, TerraPower) advancing toward 2027-2030 deployments
- Gas turbine OEMs (GE Vernova, Siemens, Mitsubishi) facing severe 5-year order backlogs
- Transformers, switchgear, and distribution equipment face 5-30% supply deficits through 2030
- Battery storage scaling rapidly (CATL 22% market share, Tesla Megapack 3, Form Energy iron-air)
- Fusion energy approaching commercial validation (CFS, Helion, TAE, General Fusion)

## KEY MARKET CONSTRAINTS & DYNAMICS (2026)

### 1. TRANSFORMER SHORTAGE PERSISTENCE
- **Current Deficit:** 5-30% shortfall (down from initial 30%)
- **Projection:** 5% deficit by 2030 (from current 30% for power transformers)
- **Supply Chain Response:**
  - Siemens: $150M North Carolina facility (2027 startup)
  - Eaton: $340M South Carolina facility (2027 startup)
  - Hitachi: $1B U.S. investment (South Boston VA 2028 startup)
- **Timeline:** Shortage persistence likely through 2030+

### 2. GAS TURBINE BOTTLENECK
- **Backlog:** 5-year order backlog extending into 2030s
- **Capacity Constraints:** Three major OEMs (GE, Siemens, Mitsubishi) represent ~66% market share
- **2026 Production:** GE/Siemens targeting 70-80 units/year; Mitsubishi doubling capacity
- **Market Reality:** Supply remains insufficient for datacenter buildout pace
- **Implication:** Gas-powered datacenters will face multi-year delays

### 3. NUCLEAR FUEL SUPPLY CHAIN TIGHTNESS
- **U.S. Enrichment Gap:** Supply deficit projected for late 2020s until 2030s capacity online
- **Key Players Scaling:**
  - URENCO: 2.5M SWU new capacity; 10% enrichment authorization
  - Orano: $5B enrichment facility investment; NRC application H1 2026
  - Centrus: Only U.S. HALEU producer (critical for advanced reactors)
- **Global Supply:** Kazatomprom (40%), Cameco (14-20%), Others (40%)
- **2026 Outlook:** Tightening supply supporting higher uranium prices

### 4. SMR/ADVANCED NUCLEAR TIMELINE REALITY
- **Operational Status:** No U.S. SMRs operational as of April 2026
- **Expected Deployments:**
  - 2027-2028: Oklo Aurora first production
  - 2028-2030: NuScale, Kairos, TerraPower advancing
  - 2030s: Widespread SMR deployment
- **Regulatory Path:** Multi-year approval cycles; uncertain deployment timelines
- **Implication:** Datacenters cannot rely on SMRs for 2026-2028 power supply

### 5. BATTERY STORAGE RAPID SCALING
- **Market Growth:** C&I BESS market $4.5B (2026) → $21B (2036); 5x growth
- **Leading Suppliers:**
  - CATL: 22% global market share
  - Tesla: Megapack 3 ramping H2 2026
  - Form Energy: 100-hour iron-air scaling
  - Eos: 8+ GWh capacity 2026
- **Datacenter Application:** 1-4 hour backup (Tesla) + 12-100 hour duration (Form, Eos)
- **Implication:** Battery storage becoming standard datacenter infrastructure component

### 6. DATACENTER POWER DEMAND ACCELERATION
- **2023 Baseline:** 19 GW
- **2030 Projection:** 35 GW (84% growth)
- **2035 Potential:** 176 GW (9.3x growth from 2023)
- **Key Drivers:** AI model training, inference, Large Language Model deployment
- **Hyperscaler Strategy:** Securing 2-3 GW PPAs with utilities (Constellation, Vistra, NextEra, others)

### 7. REGULATORY COMPLEXITY EMERGING
- **FERC Rejection:** Talen-Amazon colocation deal rejected; complicated direct nuclear-to-DC connections
- **Winning Models:** Dominion/NE Edge (Connecticut) and TVA approaches gaining traction
- **Implications:** Direct colocation becoming regulatory challenge; slower deployment pathway
- **Industry Response:** More complex interconnection agreements required

### 8. FUSION ENERGY CAPITAL INFLUX
- **Recent Milestones:**
  - CFS: SPARC magnet ring completion target summer 2026
  - Helion: 150M C plasma achieved (Feb 2026); Microsoft contract
  - TAE: Trump Media merger (Dec 2025) for $6B capital injection
  - General Fusion: SPAC merger (mid-2026) for $335M capital
- **Status:** Most venture-backed fusion companies not yet operational
- **Commercial Timeline:** 2027-2030s for first grid-scale deployments
- **Implication:** Fusion does NOT solve datacenter power shortage in 2026-2027

## STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR INVESTMENT

### Highest Priority Plays (2026-2030 Revenue Generation)
1. **Nuclear Utilities** - Constellation, Vistra, NextEra (revenue flowing now)
2. **Gas Turbine OEMs** - GE Vernova, Siemens, Mitsubishi (severe backlog; pricing power)
3. **Transformer/Grid Equipment** - Hitachi, Eaton, Siemens (supply constraints persist)
4. **Battery Storage** - Tesla, CATL, Form Energy (rapid scaling)
5. **Gas Infrastructure** - EQT, Williams, Kinder Morgan (10 Bcf/d incremental demand)

### High-Potential but Near-Term Constrained (2027-2030)
1. **Small Modular Reactors** - NuScale, Oklo, Kairos (first revenue 2027-2030)
2. **Advanced Nuclear** - TerraPower, X-energy, Moltex (2030+ timeline)
3. **Uranium Supply** - Denison, NexGen, Cameco (supporting growing fleet demand)

### Speculative/Early-Stage (2030s+)
1. **Fusion** - CFS, Helion, TAE, General Fusion (no grid-scale revenue pre-2030)
2. **Iron-Air Batteries** - Form Energy (limited commercial deployment 2026-2028)

### Supply Chain Bottleneck Winners
1. **Transformers** - Hitachi, Eaton, Siemens (5-30% supply deficit through 2030)
2. **Gas Turbines** - GE Vernova, Siemens, Mitsubishi (5-year backlog pricing power)
3. **Switchgear/PDUs** - Schneider, ABB, Vertiv, Legrand (surging datacenter demand)
