Oracle–Bloom Energy: 2.8 GW Fuel Cell Deal

FERC Interconnection Fast-Track: Delayed to June

Energy Secretary Wright's directive for expedited datacenter grid interconnection originally had an April 30, 2026 FERC deadline, now pushed to end of June. FERC Chair Swett says reforms remain top priority.

Key proposal: allow co-located generation + grid connections simultaneously, 60-day study process (vs. years currently), and 20 MW "large load" threshold. SPP's High Impact Large Load proposal was approved at April 16 open meeting.

Investment signal: Delay adds near-term uncertainty but eventual ruling is strongly positive for CEG VST and co-location plays. Grid equipment makers ETN SU benefit either way.

🔧 Hardware & Semiconductors

Google Cloud Next: TPU v8 Dual-Track Architecture

Google announced a historic split in its TPU strategy at Cloud Next 2026:

TPU 8t "Sunfish" (training, w/ Broadcom): dual compute dies, 8× HBM3e stacks (12-high), ~30% more memory bandwidth than Ironwood. 2.8× Ironwood performance at same price.

TPU 8i "Zebrafish" (inference, w/ MediaTek): single die, 6× HBM3e stacks, 20-30% lower cost than training variant. 80% better inference performance vs Ironwood.

Both chips on TSMC 2nm, targeted late 2027. Meanwhile, Ironwood (7th gen) is now GA — 4.6 petaFLOPS/chip, 42.5 exaFLOPS in 9,216-chip superpod.

Investment signal: Major competitive threat to Nvidia's inference monopoly. Broadcom (AVGO) and MediaTek benefit from design wins. TSMC 2nm capacity becomes even more critical bottleneck.

TurboQuant: 6× Memory Reduction, 8× Speedup

New quantization breakthrough allows KV cache compression to just 3 bits with zero accuracy loss. Delivers 6× memory reduction and up to 8× faster attention computation.

Implications are massive for inference economics: lower memory = smaller/cheaper GPUs per query, dramatically reducing cost-per-token for production deployments. Could accelerate the shift from training to inference spend.
