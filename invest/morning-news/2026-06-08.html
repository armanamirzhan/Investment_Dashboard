NVIDIA inks sweeping South Korea AI deals: SK Hynix multi-year memory, SK Telecom GW-scale cloud, Naver/Doosan/LG/Hyundai partnerships — Capping Jensen Huang's Seoul visit, NVIDIA on Monday announced a multi-partner AI infrastructure package with South Korea's largest conglomerates. SK Hynix signed a multi-year technology partnership to co-develop next-generation memory for global AI datacenters, with the option to extend; Huang reiterated that 'SK Hynix has been NVIDIA's largest memory partner and will continue to be.' SK Telecom committed to building a gigawatt-scale AI cloud in Korea using NVIDIA platforms, with the first AI datacenter coming online in 2027 and phased expansion to full GW capacity thereafter. Naver and Doosan will deploy NVIDIA tech across their own AI factories — Doosan also supplying materials used in Blackwell and slating its energy-solutions stack for NVIDIA datacenter platforms. LG Group will collaborate on humanoid-robot electronics, mechanicals and physical-AI software; Hyundai will deepen autonomous-mobility and AI-powered manufacturing work. No financial terms disclosed across the agreements.

Corning surges ~10% premarket on multibillion, multi-year Amazon fiber optic deal for AI datacenters — Amazon and Corning announced a multi-year, multibillion-dollar agreement Monday for GLW to supply optical fiber, cable and connectivity solutions to power AWS's expanding US AI-datacenter footprint.
