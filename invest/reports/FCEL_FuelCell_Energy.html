Investment Thesis

FuelCell Energy is a carbonate fuel cell manufacturer making an aggressive pivot toward datacenter power generation. The company's Q1 FY2026 revenue surged 61% YoY to $30.5M, driven by a datacenter pipeline that has grown 275% since February 2025 to over 1.5 GW. The SDCL partnership targets 450 MW of identified projects, and the new standardized 12.5 MW power blocks enable deployment in months rather than years.

The unique tri-gen technology produces electricity, hydrogen, and usable heat (for absorption cooling) from a single fuel source — a compelling value proposition for datacenters that need both power and cooling. However, the company remains significantly unprofitable ($192M operating loss in FY2025, net loss $7.42/share) with a small revenue base ($158M) relative to its datacenter ambitions. The $379.6M cash position provides meaningful runway, but at-the-market dilution ($56.3M in Q1 alone) raises concerns. Backlog declined 10.8% to $1.17B as revenue recognized faster than new awards.

Datacenter Power Strategy

Core Technology: Molten carbonate fuel cells (MCFC) — stationary baseload power generation from natural gas or biogas

Tri-Gen Capability: Single fuel source produces electricity + hydrogen + usable heat; absorption cooling reduces datacenter cooling load (100 MW model increases usable power from 69.5 MW to 77.2 MW)

Standardized 12.5 MW Power Block: 10 x 1.25 MW modules with integrated absorption cooling. Deploy individually or across campuses. Months, not years.

SDCL Partnership: Collaboration with Sustainable Development Capital LLP for up to 450 MW of identified datacenter and mission-critical power projects (announced Jan 2026)

Pipeline: 1.5+ GW of datacenter proposals, 275% growth since February 2025

ExxonMobil Carbon Capture: Pilot project in Rotterdam with two modules shipping Q2 2026

Manufacturing Expansion: Torrington, CT facility planned expansion from 100 MW to 350 MW annual capacity

Source: FuelCell Energy IR press releases (investor.fce.com), Q1 FY2026 earnings call transcript

Risks & Concerns

• Declining backlog: $1.17B down 10.8% — need to convert pipeline to firm orders

• Deep operating losses: $192M operating loss in FY2025, net loss $7.42/share

• Dilution risk: $56.3M raised via at-the-market sales in Q1 2026 alone (6.4M shares)

• Scale mismatch: $158M annual revenue vs. multi-GW datacenter ambitions — long ramp ahead

• Competition: Bloom Energy (larger, more established SOFC platform), natural gas turbines, grid power

• Analyst skepticism: Average PT of $8.24 implies 36% downside from current $12.94 price

• Technology risk: Molten carbonate cells less proven for datacenter-scale deployments than SOFC

Peer Comparison

vs. Bloom Energy (BE): Bloom uses SOFC technology, has $1.2B+ revenue, established datacenter customer base (Equinix). FCEL is earlier stage but offers unique tri-gen capability and carbon capture potential.

vs. Plug Power (PLUG): Plug is larger ($710M revenue) but focused on PEM fuel cells and hydrogen infrastructure. FCEL focuses on baseload stationary power with molten carbonate technology. Different market positioning.

vs. Natural Gas: Fuel cells offer higher electrical efficiency (47-60% vs. 35-40% for turbines), lower emissions, and modular deployment. But gas turbines are proven, cheaper per kW installed, and available at larger scale today.
