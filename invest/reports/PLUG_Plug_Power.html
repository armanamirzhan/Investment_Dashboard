Investment Thesis

Plug Power is the largest pure-play hydrogen fuel cell company, pivoting from material handling (forklifts) and green hydrogen production toward datacenter backup and auxiliary power. FY2025 revenue reached $710M (+12.9% YoY), with Q4 2025 marking the company's first-ever positive gross margin — a critical inflection point. The company has signed an LOI with Stream Data Centers worth $132.5M+ and is testing fuel cell deployments with all three major hyperscalers (Microsoft, Amazon, Google).

However, the company remains deeply cash-negative ($536M operating cash outflow in 2025) with only $368.5M in unrestricted cash. Management's profitability timeline extends to end of 2028, creating significant execution and dilution risk. The stock trades near all-time lows (~$3) and Wall Street consensus price targets sit below the current price, reflecting skepticism about the path to profitability.

Datacenter Power Strategy

Core Offering: PEM fuel cell systems for datacenter backup and auxiliary power, replacing diesel generators

Advantages: Zero local emissions, low acoustic impact, no clean air permit constraints, extended runtime without grid dependency

Stream Data Centers Deal: LOI for $132.5M+ asset monetization supporting US datacenter buildout, exploring auxiliary and backup power solutions

Electricity Rights Monetization: $275M+ program supporting datacenter infrastructure investment

Hyperscaler Engagement: Initial testing with all three major hyperscalers — deployments expected "at some scale"

Cost Parity Timeline: Hydrogen fuel cells expected to reach cost parity with diesel for datacenter backup in 3-5 years

Source: Plug Power IR press releases, datacenterdynamics.com, plugpower.com/applications/stationary-power/data-centers

Risks & Concerns

• Severe cash burn: $536M operating cash outflow in 2025 with only $368M unrestricted cash — runway under 1 year at current burn

• Long path to profitability: Full profitability not expected until end of 2028

• Dilution risk: Stock near all-time lows (~$3.08); likely need for additional capital raises

• Hydrogen economics: Green hydrogen remains expensive vs. natural gas and grid power for baseload
