Investment Thesis

Vertiv is the dominant thermal management and power distribution leader for AI datacenters, positioned at the intersection of two megatrends: exploding AI compute demand and the resulting thermal challenge of 100+ kW per-rack power density. Q1 2026 delivered exceptional results: revenue of $2.65B (+30% YoY, 23% organic), adjusted EPS of $1.17 (+83% YoY), and adjusted free cash flow of $653M (+147% YoY).

FY2025 was a breakout year with $10.2B revenue (+28% YoY), $1.3B net income (+169%), and backlog surging to $15.0B (+109% YoY). The Q4 2025 book-to-bill ratio of 2.9x with 252% organic orders growth signals massive forward demand. The company has raised 2026 guidance to $13.5-14.0B revenue with adjusted operating margin of 22.8-23.8% and adjusted EPS of $6.30-6.40.

Key differentiators include CoolPhase Flex hybrid cooling (air + liquid in one system), 800VDC power distribution for NVIDIA next-gen GPU racks, and a global service network. Net leverage at 0.2x with $5.0B liquidity provides financial flexibility for both organic investment and M&A.

Datacenter Infrastructure Strategy

Core Products: Thermal management (precision cooling, liquid cooling), power distribution (UPS, PDUs, switchgear, busbar), IT management software (Trellis, Vertiv Intelligence)

Liquid Cooling: CoolPhase Flex — first-of-its-kind hybrid solution combining refrigerant-based air and liquid cooling in a single flexible system. Critical for 100+ kW per-rack AI GPU deployments.

800VDC Power: Next-generation power distribution architecture designed specifically for NVIDIA AI GPU racks — eliminates AC/DC conversion losses, improves efficiency

Customer Base: All major hyperscalers (MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL, META), colocation providers, enterprise datacenters globally

Market Position: #1 or #2 in all core product categories globally. Orders growth broad-based across Americas, EMEA, and APAC regions

STL Acquisition: Strategic acquisition to strengthen integrated power and cooling portfolio

Source: Vertiv Q1 2026 earnings call, investor presentations via investors.vertiv.com

Risks & Concerns

• Valuation: ~42x forward P/E and $131B market cap — stock trading near analyst avg PT of $339, priced for sustained execution

• Hyperscaler concentration: Top customers drive outsized revenue share — any single customer pullback would be felt

• Execution risk: Delivering 34% revenue growth with simultaneous 290bps margin expansion is demanding

• STL integration: Recent acquisition needs to be integrated while organic growth demands full management attention

• Cycle risk: If hyperscaler capex cycle decelerates (e.g., Microsoft's 1.5 GW freeze), demand could moderate

• Competition: Schneider Electric, Eaton, and niche liquid cooling players competing for the same hyperscaler budgets

Peer Comparison

vs. Eaton (ETN): Both are critical datacenter infrastructure plays but Eaton focuses on electrical distribution (switchgear, transformers, PDUs) while Vertiv leads in thermal management. Vertiv has higher DC revenue exposure (45-50% vs Eaton's ~25%) and faster revenue growth (+30% vs +12.9%), but trades at a higher P/E multiple. Eaton's datacenter backlog is 11 years.

vs. Schneider Electric (SNEXF): Schneider competes across UPS, PDUs, and building management but Vertiv has stronger positioning in next-gen liquid cooling. Schneider is more diversified (industrial automation, building efficiency) while Vertiv is a purer datacenter play.

vs. niche liquid cooling (Asetek, CoolIT): Vertiv's CoolPhase Flex hybrid approach offers flexibility that pure-play liquid cooling companies lack. Scale, global service network, and hyperscaler relationships are significant moats.
