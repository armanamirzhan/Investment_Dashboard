NVDA — China greenlights limited H200 sales to Alibaba, ByteDance, DeepSeek; ceiling ~200k chips, training-only, per Information/Bloomberg — Bloomberg confirmed Wednesday July 8 (citing The Information) that China's cyber-space regulators have informed Alibaba (BABA), ByteDance and DeepSeek they will be permitted to purchase a limited quantity of Nvidia H200 training accelerators. Approved companies must submit chip-count and use-case justifications; total approvals are being capped at under 200,000 units, less than half the aggregate requested earlier in 2026. Approved allocations are restricted to AI-model training — inference workloads must run on domestically produced silicon (Huawei Ascend). The mechanic layers onto the existing U.S. framework: Trump's Dec 8, 2025 announcement conditionally cleared H200/MI325X-class exports; BIS issued the implementing rule Jan 13, 2026 (effective Jan 16), with a 25% U.S.-side revenue-share fee and third-party performance testing required. Nvidia now sits on both approvals — U.S. issuer license + Chinese purchaser license — for the first time. The Information's April 2026 reporting had pegged Chinese demand at 400-500k units through 2027, so the 200k cap approximates 40-50% of that TAM. Reaction: NVDA +3.65% Wednesday close to $204.12; pre-market Thursday +0.6% to $205.30. BofA reiterated Buy and framed the setup as 'enhanced Buy opp'ty at 7-yr low 18x fwd PE.' Read-through: this is the single largest positive datapoint on the China overhang since the January framework took effect; every incremental Chinese-approved SKU is directly incremental to NVDA and AMD's data-center TAM. Downside: Beijing kept discretion to tighten or freeze approvals; the U.S. 25% revenue-share still applies to shipped units.
