Investment Thesis

Bloom Energy is the clear market leader in stationary fuel cells for datacenters, and has emerged as one of the most consequential power infrastructure plays of the AI buildout cycle. Q1 2026 was transformational: revenue surged 130% YoY to $751M (record quarter), the company achieved its first GAAP net income ($70.7M), and Oracle expanded its master agreement to 2.8 GW — making it the largest single fuel cell procurement in history.

FY2025 revenue reached $2.02B (+37% YoY) and 2026 guidance has been raised to $3.4-3.8B (~80% growth). The key differentiator is deployment speed: Bloom delivered a fully operational system to Oracle in 55 days, over a month ahead of schedule. For grid-constrained datacenter sites where utility connections take 3-5 years, this is a game-changer. Gross margins have expanded to 31.5% and the company is now generating free cash flow ($57M in FY2025).

Key risks center on valuation — the stock is up ~240% YTD and trades at $82B market cap, above analyst consensus targets — and Oracle customer concentration in the near term.

Datacenter Power Strategy

Technology: Solid Oxide Fuel Cell (SOFC) — modular 325 kW building blocks scalable to 100+ MW

Fuel Flexibility: Natural gas, hydrogen, biogas — enables transition path to clean hydrogen

Key Advantage: Non-combustion direct electricity conversion; 55-day deployment (vs. 3-5 years for utility grid connections)

Oracle Partnership: Master agreement expanded to 2.8 GW — initial 1.2 GW contracted, deployment underway. 55-day installation delivered a month ahead of 90-day schedule.

Existing Customers: Equinix (South San Jose datacenter), Oracle, and growing pipeline

Advantages over Diesel: Zero local NOx/SOx emissions, continuous baseload power (not just backup), lower noise, no clean air permit constraints

Source: Bloom Energy IR press releases, Oracle partnership announcements via investor.bloomenergy.com

Risks & Concerns

• Valuation: Stock up ~240% YTD to $82B market cap — trading above $234 analyst avg PT

• Customer concentration: Oracle dominates near-term revenue pipeline

• Leverage: $2.6B long-term debt vs $2.5B cash — net debt position

• Competition: Gas turbines proven and cheaper per kW for large-scale baseload

• Natural gas dependency: Current systems run primarily on natural gas — not zero-carbon without hydrogen transition

• Scale limits: Modular approach excellent for grid-constrained sites but may not compete with utility-scale solutions for greenfield builds

Peer Comparison

vs. Plug Power (PLUG): Bloom is far more advanced commercially ($2B+ revenue vs $710M), profitable in Q1 2026, and has major datacenter deployments underway. PLUG focuses on PEM fuel cells and is earlier-stage in datacenter strategy.

vs. FuelCell Energy (FCEL): Bloom has ~13x FCEL's revenue and proven datacenter deployments. FCEL's tri-gen offering (power + hydrogen + cooling) is differentiated but unproven at scale. Bloom's SOFC technology has a clear commercial lead.

vs. Gas Turbines (GE Vernova, Siemens Energy): Fuel cells offer faster deployment and lower emissions but higher cost per kW. Gas turbines dominate large-scale baseload. Fuel cells excel in grid-constrained, behind-the-meter applications.
